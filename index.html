VINCENT VAN GOGH

The tortured artist

A painting of Van Gogh by the Australian artist John Peter Russell, 1886

Vincent Van Gogh was a Dutch post-impressionist painter who lived from 1853 to 1890. He is widely considered to be one of the most important artists in the history of Western art.

Having only started painting at age 27, Van Gogh created over two thousand artworks in the ten years before he died, most of which were made in the last two years of his life. Van Gogh's work was characterized by his use of bold, vibrant colors, thick impasto, and dramatic, swirling brushstrokes.

He is remembered as a tragic figure, having died by suicide at the young age of 37, but his legacy lives on as one of the most important and influential artists of all time. This is his story.

Timeline of Van Gogh's life:

  • 1853: Vincent Willem Van Gogh is born on March 30 in the Netherlands
  • 1869: He leaves school and starts working for an art dealer in The Hague.
  • 1873: Van Gogh is transferred to the London branch of the art dealership.
  • 1875: He leaves the art dealership and takes unpaid work as a schoolteacher in England.
  • 1876: Van Gogh returns to the Netherlands and works as a bookstore clerk.
  • 1877: He begins to study theology in Amsterdam, but does not complete the course.
  • 1878: Van Gogh becomes a Protestant missionary in a predominantly Roman Catholic district of Belgium.
  • 1880: He decides to become an artist and moves to Brussels to study at the Académie Royale des Beaux-Arts. His younger brother Theo supported him financially; the two kept a long correspondence by letter - this continued up to the end of Van Gogh's life.
  • 1886: Van Gogh moves to Paris to live with his brother Theo, and becomes influenced by the Impressionist and Post-Impressionist art movements.
  • 1888: He moves to Arles in the south of France, where he hopes to establish an artists' colony. During this time he produces some of his most famous works. He continued to suffer from mental breakdowns and distress, the worst of which led to him severing his left ear with a razor.
  • 1889: Van Gogh voluntarily enters a psychiatric hospital in Arles and later in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence and Saint-Paul-de-Mausole, where he continues to paint. He often used the asylums and their surrounding nature as inspiration for his works. Around this time, he wrote of "...moods of indescribable anguish, sometimes moments when the veil of time and fatality of circumstances seemed to be torn apart for an instant"
  • 1890: He leaves the hospital and moves to Auvers-sur-Oise near Paris, where he is under the care of a homeopathic doctor. From 20 March to 27 April 1890, Van Gogh was included in the sixth exhibition of the Société des Artistes Indépendants in the Pavillon de la Ville de Paris on the Champs-Elysées - his paintings were said by Claude Monet to be the best in the showVan Gogh dies from a self-inflicted gunshot wound on July 29, 1890, at the age of 37. His final painting, Wheatfield with Crows was made just a few hours before his death. According to his brother Theo, Vincent's last words were "The sadness will last forever."
