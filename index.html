VINCENT VAN GOGH

The tortured artist

A painting of Van Gogh by the Australian artist John Peter Russell, 1886

Vincent Van Gogh was a Dutch post-impressionist painter who lived from 1853 to 1890. He is widely considered to be one of the most important artists in the history of Western art.

Having only started painting at age 27, Van Gogh created over two thousand artworks in the ten years before he died, most of which were made in the last two years of his life. Van Gogh's work was characterized by his use of bold, vibrant colors, thick impasto, and dramatic, swirling brushstrokes.

He is remembered as a tragic figure, having died by suicide at the young age of 37, but his legacy lives on as one of the most important and influential artists of all time. This is his story.
